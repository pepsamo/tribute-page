Linus Torvalds

the creator and the principal developer of Linux and Git

Here's a time line of Linus Torvalds:

  • 1969 - Born in Helsinki, Finland
  • 1988 to 1966 - Studied at the University of Helsinki
  • 1991 - Purchased the Intel 80386-based IBM PC and obtained a MINIX operating system. With this, he began to work and research on the Linux operating system.
  • 1996 - Went on a visit to the technology firm, ‘Transmeta’, where he began to work from the following year. He later worked for the Open Source Development Labs.
  • 1997 to 1999 - From 1997 to 1999, he worked on the '86open' project, which is a binary file format for the ‘Unix’ and Unix-like operating systems. They were to be used on a common x86 PC.
  • 1998 - Was a recipient of the EFF Pioneer Award.
  • 1999 - Was offered stock options for his creation by the companies, Red Hat and VA Linux. That very year, the stock prices increased to about $20 million after the companies went public and he was given the title of 'one of the top 100 innovators, under the age of 35, in the world' by MIT Technology Review TR100.
  • 2000 - Was the recipient of the Lovelace Medal, which was conferred by the British Computer Society.
  • 2001 - Along with Richard Stallman and Ken Sakamura, he shared the Takeda Award for Social/Economic Well-Being.
  • 2005 - Developed the software, ‘Git’.
  • 2008 - Become an inductee into the Hall of Fellows of the Computer History Museum in Mountain View, California.
  • 2010 - Was awarded the C&C Prize by the NEC Corporation.
  • 2012 - Stated that his contribution to the Linux kernel mostly involved merging codes that were already written by others and did not involve much programming. Was inducted into the Internet Hall of Fame and was one of the recipients of the Millennium Technology Prize.
